November 22, 2024

Tycho Crater

Observed during the lunar evening, the dramatic rays of Tycho were spectacular. The central peak was clearly visible with excellent seeing conditions.

Object Data

Object Type: Lunar Crater

Constellation: N/A

Size: 85 km diameter

Location: Backyard, Austin, TX

Equipment: 8" Dobsonian, 10mm eyepiece

Conditions: Clear, Moon 8 days old, seeing 4/5

Visual Description

The crater appeared as a brilliant white splash with rays extending in all directions across the lunar surface. The terraced walls were well-defined, and the central peak cast a sharp shadow toward the crater floor.

Notes

  • Best observed during lunar evening (days 8-10 after new moon)
  • Ray system extends over 1,500 km in some directions
  • Central peak height estimated at 2 km above crater floor
